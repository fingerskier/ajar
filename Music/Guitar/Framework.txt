Framework

Dependencies:
	memorizing notes on the fretboard
		start with string 6, then progress
	it is highly advisable to facilitate the framework via ear-training

Philosophy:
	all music revolves around these horizontal and vertical patterns
	playability demands vertical movements, tonality demands horizontal movements - a balance must be struck
	slow is smooth, smooth is fast
		the primary goal of all practice is produce a solid, deliberate tone
		the secondary goal is to maintain this tone while harmonizing and making the melody
		the tertiary goal is to synthesize the music until it can flow effortlessly (i.e. quickly)
	performance precedence
		1st tone
			clear, clean notes are always best
		2nd projection
			it is possible to play pianissimo at high volume
		3rd speed
			high-speed play is a desired end result, never a goal
	ideally we do not memorize notes, but named patterns - learn the patterns and how to describe them using musical terminology

	memorization
		Of utmost importance is memorizing all of the notes on the entire fingerboard.
		Then, you need only memorize 6 chords shapes and five scales.
		Then, you can discover how chords and scales fit together in common phrases.
		Then, memorized music becomes a series of variations of the phrases - each of which have an address in your brain.

Notation
	This is non-standard and really only lends itself to guitar but this is how I often notate my music (I even these on my performance copy of sheet music oftentimes.)
	
	Granted this says nothing about the rhythm but it tells us succintly how the place the fingers and hand.

	Numbers following the chord name indicate key tones.

	Letters following the chord name indicate mode tones.

	Example 1: VII-B-453
		this a B chord in the seventh position with an arpeggiation across the 4th, 5th, and 3rd tones of the scale.

	Example 2: II-D-kij
		this a D chord in the second position with an arpeggiation across the 5th, 1st, and root tones of the chord.
		
	Example 3: a/7E\be
		E chord played in the seventh position, inside bar to cover an A in the bass and lave the E & B open
